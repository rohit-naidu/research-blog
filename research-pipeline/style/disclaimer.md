## A note on what this article is and isn't

This is research synthesis, not medical advice. We are researchers and
operators, not your clinicians. Everything here is based on published
evidence and patient-reported experience, but your body, your other
medications, and your medical history are specific to you in ways no
blog post can account for.

Before starting, stopping, or changing the dose of any medication,
supplement, or protocol mentioned in this article, talk to the prescriber
who manages your GLP-1 therapy. If you don't have one, get one. The
specific interactions between GLP-1 receptor agonists and other drugs
(including OTC supplements) are real and sometimes serious - and they
are exactly the kind of thing a real prescriber catches and a blog
cannot.

If you experience severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, signs of
pancreatitis, signs of an allergic reaction, or any other symptom that
feels dangerous, stop reading this article and call your doctor or go
to an emergency room.

We work on this because we believe most GLP-1 side effects are
preventable or manageable, not because we believe articles like this
one substitute for medical care.
